1990s

The 1990s were a pivotal decade for technology, marking the transition from analog to digital. The rise of personal computers and the internet laid the groundwork for the digital age. During this decade, technology began to penetrate everyday life, moving from business and academia into homes worldwide. Personal computing became more accessible, with graphical user interfaces making technology easier to use. The internet emerged as a powerful tool for communication and information, leading to a profound transformation in how people interacted with the world around them.

1991

1991

Introduction of the World Wide Web

Tim Berners-Lee introduced the World Wide Web, providing a new way to access and share information. This breakthrough laid the foundation for the internet as we know it today, transforming communication, commerce, and entertainment.

1993

1993

Mosaic Browser Launch

The Mosaic web browser made the internet more accessible with its graphical user interface. It paved the way for the widespread adoption of the web, leading to the development of future browsers and internet technologies.

1995

1995

Windows 95 Release

Microsoft launched Windows 95, which featured a user-friendly interface and became a cornerstone in personal computing. It brought the PC into millions of homes and businesses, marking a significant step in the digital transition.

1997

1997

Introduction of DVDs

The introduction of DVDs marked a significant advancement in digital storage, offering greater capacity than CDs and becoming a popular medium for movies and data storage

2000s

The 2000s saw the internet become integral to daily life, with significant advancements in digital connectivity and the beginning of the mobile revolution. Despite the volatility of the dot-com bubble burst, the internet continued to grow in importance. Broadband internet became widespread, transforming how people accessed information and entertainment. The rise of social media began to change the landscape of communication, and the introduction of smartphones revolutionized mobile technology, making the internet portable and ubiquitous. These advancements laid the groundwork for the interconnected, always-online world we live in today.

2000

2000

Y2K and the Dot-Com Boom

The Y2K scare highlighted the reliance on digital systems, and the dot-com boom showed the potential and volatility of internet-based businesses. Despite the bubble burst, the internet's role continued to grow

2001

2001

Broadband Internet Expansion

The expansion of broadband internet brought faster and more reliable connections, enabling the growth of rich online content, streaming services, and more sophisticated web applications.

2004

2004

Facebook Launches

Mark Zuckerberg launched Facebook, a social networking site initially aimed at college students. Facebook rapidly grew, changing the way people connect and communicate online, and setting the stage for the rise of social media platforms.

2007

2007

Release of the First iPhone

Apple's iPhone introduced a revolutionary device that combined phone, iPod, and internet communication. Its innovative touchscreen interface and app ecosystem sparked the smartphone revolution.

In the 1990s, the internet revolution began in earnest with the public launch of the World Wide Web, turning a network of academic and military computers into a global communications powerhouse that reshaped society.

2010s

The 2010s were defined by the rapid adoption of smart technology, as devices became increasingly interconnected and social media revolutionized communication. This decade witnessed the widespread use of smartphones, tablets, and wearables, integrating technology into everyday life more personally. Social media platforms expanded massively, altering how people connect, share, and consume information. The introduction of smart home devices and voice-activated assistants added convenience and automation to daily routines. Additionally, progress in electric vehicles and renewable energy began addressing environmental issues, setting the stage for a sustainable future.

2012
